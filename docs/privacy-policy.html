SmackTrack Privacy Policy

Golf Shot Distance Tracker

Last updated: February 22, 2026

What the app does

SmackTrack uses your phone's GPS to measure how far you hit a golf ball. That's it.

Location data

  • GPS is used only to calculate the distance between your start and end positions on the golf course.
  • Your coordinates are never transmitted, uploaded, or shared with any server or third party.
  • Raw GPS coordinates are not stored. Only the calculated distance in yards/meters is saved to your shot history.

Weather data

  • When a shot is recorded, the app sends your approximate location to the Open-Meteo weather API to fetch current wind and temperature conditions.
  • Open-Meteo is a free, open-source weather API. Their privacy policy is available on their website.
  • No other network requests are made by the app.

Data stored on your device

  • Shot history: Club used, distance, weather conditions, and timestamp.
  • Settings: Your preferred units (yards/meters, mph/km/h, °F/°C), trajectory, and club selections.
  • All data is stored locally on your device using Android SharedPreferences.
  • No data is stored on external servers or in the cloud.

Data we collect

None. We do not collect, track, or transmit any personal information. There are no analytics, no advertising SDKs, no user accounts, and no telemetry.

Third-party services

  • Open-Meteo — weather data only (no personal data sent, just coordinates for the weather lookup).
  • Google Play Services — used for GPS location via FusedLocationProviderClient.

No advertising networks, analytics platforms, or social media SDKs are included in the app.

Children's privacy

The app does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, the changes will be posted on this page with a new "last updated" date.
